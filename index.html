Research Directions
01
Adversarial Self-Play in Alignment
Most alignment methods teach models what to say. AdverSplay-GRPO teaches models what not to hide. The framework pits a sycophancy-generating adversary against an honest responder in a minimax loop — using dual LoRA adapters on a frozen Qwen3-32B base with Group Relative Policy Optimisation.

Result: 89.3% sycophancy reduction. The adversarial training surface forces the model to develop genuinely robust honest reasoning rather than surface-level compliance.
Breaking the Mirror · COLM 2026
02
Mechanistic Origins of Specification Gaming
When a model games its objective, what is the circuit-level computation that produces that behaviour? Joint work with Amirali Abdullah (Thoughtworks) using activation patching, attention head ablation, and causal tracing on transformer models exhibiting reward hacking patterns.

The goal: find the internal features that make specification gaming possible, then understand whether they can be surgically removed without destroying the underlying capability.
ACL 2026 · Under Review
03
Belief Geometry in Deep RL
Collaboration with Prof. Fernando Rosas (University of Sussex) through the MARS 4.0 program at the Cambridge AI Safety Hub. We study how belief-state representations form geometrically across training — and what their structure reveals about the agent's implicit world model.

Key finding: R² > 0.993 belief-state decodability from latent representations, suggesting that internal belief structure is far more legible than previously assumed.
RLC 2026 · Under Review
04
Structured Ignorance Certificates
Models fail silently. The problem is not just that they are wrong — it is that they do not know they are wrong in a structured, auditable way. This project investigates how to make models produce explicit, verifiable representations of their own epistemic boundaries.

Running experiments on Qwen3-14B on Lambda Labs A100 infrastructure. Targeting EIML @ ICML 2026.

Governance Research
The Headless Agent Problem in Military AI
arXiv:2603.03515
Alignment failures that arise not from model internals but from the absence of a coherent decision-making authority above the model. When no human is meaningfully in the loop, the alignment problem changes shape entirely.
Propaganda is an Objective, Not Content
ICML Agents in the Wild Workshop · In Preparation
The standard framing of AI propaganda detection treats propaganda as a content category. This paper argues it is better understood as an optimisation objective — which changes what defences are possible.
The Containment Fallacy
In Progress
Alignment methods cannot remove Machiavellian knowledge from model weights through fine-tuning alone. The knowledge persists under distributional shift even when surface behaviour changes. This paper formalises why containment is structurally insufficient as an alignment strategy.
SAHOO: Recursive Self-Improvement Under Alignment Constraints
arXiv:2603.06333 · GitHub: SubramanyamSahoo/SAHOO-in-Recursive-Self-Improvement
A framework for studying how alignment constraints interact with recursive self-improvement dynamics — what breaks, what holds, and what new failure modes emerge at scale.

Who I Am

I am an independent AI safety researcher based in Cuttack, Odisha, India. My work sits at the intersection of alignment science, mechanistic interpretability, and AI governance — with a specific focus on what breaks alignment under adversarial conditions and why.

Before independent research I completed an M.Tech in Artificial Intelligence at NIT Hamirpur, graduating as gold medalist and department topper under Dr. Kamlesh Dutta. Prior to that, a B.Tech in Computer Science from Parala Maharaja Engineering College under Dr. Debasis Mohapatra.

I work with Prof. Fernando Rosas (University of Sussex) on belief geometry in deep RL through the MARS 4.0 program at the Cambridge AI Safety Hub, and with Amirali Abdullah (Thoughtworks) on mechanistic interpretability.
